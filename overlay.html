Rehwa SocietyRehwa Society, founded in 1979 by Richard and Sally
Holkar in Maheshwar, supports traditional Maheshwari
weaving, growing from 12 women to 250 weavers. Profits
fund welfare initiatives, preserving culture and livelihoods
while showcasing their craft through exhibitions. The
organization also invests in education, housing, and
healthcare for the weavers, ensuring community well
-being.

Gudi MudiEstablished in 2003 in Maheshwar, Women Weavers
empowers local women by making handloom weaving
a dignified livelihood. The Charitable Trust provides skill
training and connects weavers with retailers, designers,
and customers across India and internationally, promoting
sustainable livelihoods and high-quality products.

Old HousesThe old houses of Maheshwar, rich in heritage, have
been transformed into business hubs for weaving
workshops and local crafts. These spaces preserve the
town's cultural legacy while supporting sustainable
livelihoods for artisans and attracting global customers.
